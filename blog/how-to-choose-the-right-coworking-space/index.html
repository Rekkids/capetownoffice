How to choose the right coworking space for your needs

← All posts

Choosing a coworking space involves more than admiring glossy photos online. It takes a practical look at how you actually work, and what your work pattern really demands. In Cape Town, where the coworking landscape is as varied as its scenery, the decision should start with work pattern, then infrastructure, then location, then aesthetics, in that order.

Decision criteria for selecting a workspace

Start with your work pattern

Before you get drawn in by sleek furniture or a hipster vibe, consider how you work. Are you a digital nomad needing odd hours and stable internet, or a local consultant who needs meeting rooms and a quiet space for client calls? Understanding your work pattern helps you focus on the parts of a coworking space that actually affect your daily productivity.

Compare access hours and infrastructure

Reliable internet isn't a perk, it's essential, and sometimes it goes down. What matters is how quickly issues get resolved and what precautions are in place. Dedicated Ethernet/LAN points are one of the overlooked details that make the difference between a frustrating, hours-long upload and a seamless one. Look for a space that matches your access needs, whether that's 24/7 availability or consistently fast internet.

Check commute, parking, and neighbourhood fit

Location matters in a city like Cape Town, where traffic can be unpredictable. Consider the commute, and whether parking's available if you're driving. Cape Town Office is centrally located but doesn't have in-building parking, which might be a dealbreaker for some, though there's a good choice of free on-street parking nearby. Choose a neighbourhood that fits your lifestyle. Good gyms, coffee shops, restaurants and shops within walking distance count for more than people expect.

Compare membership models

Not all coworking spaces offer the same flexibility. Whether you need a day pass or a dedicated desk, compare membership models based on how often you'll actually use the space and your budget, and whether you can scale up or down as your needs change. We've observed how people actually work, and spoiler alert: we don't all work a standard 9-5. So we created a solution for people like this, where you pay for the actual time you use. And the best part -- no app to download, no manual check-in, no tracking your own hours. Take a look at our calculator to see what fits.

Book a tour

A physical tour tells you things online reviews and descriptions can't. Test the WiFi, observe the atmosphere, chat with current members about their experience. Finding parking, having a coffee, meeting a few people who actually work there, that's what really helps you decide.
